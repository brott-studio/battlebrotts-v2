## Why defer, not revise-#3 tonight

Revision-2 §6 explicitly names the escalation ladder:
> "Tuning-level adjustment is a constant change and stays in this sprint. Design-level anomaly triggers a pause, escalation to Ett/HCD, and probable scope reduction to Option C."

Pass #1 confirmed structural cause (polarity preserved, lever moved the failure between chassis rather than fixing it). Pass #2 on a stacked constant would be a shot in the dark. A design revision #3 would need to address either:

1. **Sequential RNG consumption ordering** for `orbit_direction` draws (deterministic but side-asymmetric under smoothing — the two bots' draws aren't independent in effect across transition geometry).
2. **Smoothing initial-condition symmetry** (first-tick velocity state setup per side in mirror matches).

Either change is a **behavioral design decision** about how the combat sim seeds mirror-matches, not a combat-feel tuning. It has knock-on effects across every multi-bot test in the suite (any test that consumes `rng` for `orbit_direction` — likely `test_sprint13_3`, `test_sprint11`, `test_sprint11_2`, `test_sprint14_1_nav`, probably others). That's HCD-visible scope territory, not overnight-orchestration territory.
